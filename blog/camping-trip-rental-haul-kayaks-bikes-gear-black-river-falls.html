Camping & Outdoor Gear: The Right Rental to Haul It All

Kayaks, bikes, coolers, tents, the dog. Summer adventure generates a mountain of gear — here's the vehicle that swallows it whole.

Western Wisconsin is built for summer adventure. The Black River for paddling, the bluff country for biking, state parks and campgrounds in every direction, the Mississippi a short drive west. The problem is never finding somewhere to go — it's fitting all the gear in the vehicle to get there.

If your everyday car can't swallow the kayaks, the bikes, the cooler, the tent, and the people all at once, a rental from Riverside Auto Rental in Black River Falls bridges the gap. Here's how to pick the right one and pack it smart.

What actually fits where

7-passenger minivan, third row folded. This is the camping sweet spot for most families. Seats five, and the folded third row opens up a deep cargo well. Realistic load: tent, sleeping bags for the family, a big cooler, a tote of camp kitchen gear, and two bikes with front wheels off. Shorter kayaks and inflatables slide in too.

15-passenger van, rear rows configured for cargo. Now you're hauling an expedition. Drop the back two rows and you've got room for several bikes standing up, multiple coolers, a full camp setup, and gear for a group — while still seating the front rows. This is the multi-family-cabin-trip vehicle.

The roof question. Long hard-shell kayaks and canoes usually want a roof rack. If that's your situation, call before you reserve — we'll talk through what works with the vehicle so you're not improvising in the parking lot.

Smart packing for a gear-heavy trip

  • Load heavy and low first. Coolers and water jugs on the floor, gear stacked on top. Keeps the center of gravity sane on the highway.
  • Bikes: front wheels off. Pulling the front wheel off each bike turns an impossible Tetris game into an easy one. Lay them flat or lean them against the side wall.
  • Soft bags over hard cases. Duffels and dry bags flex into gaps that hard-shell luggage can't. Pack soft where you can.
  • Keep the day-of stuff reachable. Snacks, sunscreen, the first-aid kit, the leash — pack those last so they're by the door.
  • A cheap tarp or moving blanket over the cargo floor catches mud and the inevitable cooler drip, and makes return cleanup a two-minute job.

Why rent instead of cramming your own car

Save your own vehicle the wear. Hauling sandy, wet, muddy gear is hard on a personal car. A rental takes the abuse and goes back when you're done.

Right-size for the trip. You don't own a van, but you need one for this weekend. Renting gives you the big hauler for exactly the days you need it.

Mileage handled. Camping trips rack up miles to trailheads and put-ins. Our van weekly rate includes unlimited mileage; for cars, the unlimited tier covers you. See the weekly rate guide.

A note on returning the vehicle

Camping gets things dirty — that's expected and totally fine. We just ask you return the vehicle reasonably clean: shake out the worst of the dirt, pull the trash, wipe up any big spills. A few minutes at the end of the trip keeps everything simple and avoids cleaning fees. The tarp trick above makes this easy.
