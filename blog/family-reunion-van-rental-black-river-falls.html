Family Reunion Transportation: One Van, Everyone Together

The reunion's planned, the date's set, relatives are coming from everywhere. The one thing that quietly makes or breaks the weekend? How everyone gets around once they're here.

Every family reunion has an unsung hero: the person who figures out transportation. Grandma flew into Eau Claire, the cousins drove in from Chicago, Uncle Dave's rental is too small for the group dinner, and somehow everyone needs to get to the park, the restaurant, and back to the hotel — ideally without a six-car convoy losing half its members at every stoplight.

One 15-passenger van solves the whole thing. At Riverside Auto Rental — the independent, family-owned rental in Black River Falls since 1983 — reunions are one of our favorite reasons people call. Here's how to make the transportation the easy part of your reunion.

Why one van beats everyone driving separately

  • Everyone arrives together. No "we'll meet you there" that turns into 40 minutes of waiting. The group moves as one.
  • The reunion starts in the van. Some of the best catching-up happens on the drive — three generations in one vehicle, not scattered across a parking lot.
  • One designated driver. Reunions involve toasts. One sober driver in a van means everyone else relaxes.
  • Grandparents ride easy. No climbing in and out of multiple cars. One comfortable vehicle with room to spread out.
  • Parking once. Anyone who's tried to park six cars together at a busy park or restaurant knows why this matters.

The classic reunion uses for the van

Airport shuttle runs. Relatives flying into Eau Claire (50 min), La Crosse (50 min), or Minneapolis-St. Paul (2 hrs) need pickups, often on different days. The van handles multiple arrivals and their luggage in one trip.

The group outing. Whether it's a state park, a winery, a ballgame, or the lake, getting 12 people there together is effortless with the van and a willing driver.

The big dinner. Reunion dinners are often at a restaurant nobody's been to, 20 minutes away. One van, everybody arrives on time, nobody gets lost.

Hotel-to-event shuttling. If the family's split across a couple hotels, the van becomes the reunion's shuttle bus for the weekend.

The scarcity factor — book early

Here's the thing most reunion planners don't realize until it's too late: 15-passenger vans are hard to find. Most national rental chains stopped carrying them years ago. Riverside Auto Rental is one of the few providers in western Wisconsin that still has them — and summer is peak season for reunions, weddings, and group travel all at once.

Our booking guidance:

  • Regular summer weekend: book 3–4 weeks ahead
  • Holiday-weekend reunion (July 4th, Labor Day): book 4–6 weeks ahead
  • As soon as your dates are firm: reserve. No deposit required to hold the van.

Logistics: a simple reunion plan

Pick up the day before. Grab the van the day before the main event so it's ready for early airport runs and morning gatherings.

Designate one or two drivers. A standard license is all that's needed — no special endorsement for family use. We'll walk the driver through the van's size at pickup.

Return after the weekend. A Friday-to-Monday or Friday-to-Tuesday window covers most reunions and fits neatly against the weekly rate.
