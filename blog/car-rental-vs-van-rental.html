Car Rental vs. Van Rental: How to Choose

A practical guide to picking the right vehicle class for your trip, group size, and budget.

Riverside Auto Rental offers four vehicle classes — from a fuel-efficient intermediate car to a 15-passenger van that can move an entire sports team. The right choice depends on three things: how many people are going, how much stuff you're bringing, and what makes financial sense.

When to rent a car (intermediate or full-size)

A rental car is the right call when:

  • You're 1–5 people — Both car classes seat 5 comfortably.
  • Your car is in the shop — A sedan is the most affordable way to stay mobile while repairs happen.
  • You want the lowest daily rate — Starting at $55/day for local use, cars are the budget option.
  • Fuel efficiency matters — Up to 37 MPG on the intermediate car means lower gas costs, especially on longer drives.
  • You're a solo traveler or couple visiting the area — Compact, easy to park, and affordable.

Intermediate vs. Full-Size: The intermediate car is smaller and more fuel-efficient — ideal for commuting and short trips. The full-size car offers more legroom, trunk space, and comfort for longer trips or families with luggage. The daily price difference is just $10.

When to rent a minivan (7 passengers)

The 7-passenger minivan hits a sweet spot for families and small groups:

  • 6–7 people — It's the only way to keep everyone in one vehicle without going to a full-size van.
  • Families with kids — Sliding doors make loading car seats easy. There's room for strollers and luggage behind the third row.
  • Airport runs — Heading to MSP, La Crosse, or Eau Claire to pick up or drop off family? The minivan fits everyone plus luggage.
  • You need a car-equivalent replacement for a family vehicle — Insurance adjusters often authorize a minivan when the damaged vehicle was an SUV or van.

When to rent a 15-passenger van

The 15-passenger van is a specialty vehicle that most rental agencies in the area simply don't offer. It makes sense when:

  • Your group is 8+ people — No car or minivan can handle this. One van keeps everyone together.
  • Sports teams — Traveling to tournaments, games, or practice facilities with the whole squad.
  • Church groups — Youth retreats, volunteer trips, community outings.
  • Family reunions — Picking up relatives from the airport or shuttling between event locations.
  • Wedding parties — Moving the bridal party, groomsmen, or guests between venues.
  • Tourist groups — ATV trail trips, State Forest excursions, or Driftless Region road trips with a large group.
