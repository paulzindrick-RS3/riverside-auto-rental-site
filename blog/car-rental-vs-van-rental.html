Car Rental vs. Van Rental: How to Choose

A practical guide to picking the right vehicle class for your trip, group size, and budget.

Riverside Auto Rental offers four vehicle classes — from a fuel-efficient intermediate car to a 15-passenger van that can move an entire sports team. The right choice depends on three things: how many people are going, how much stuff you're bringing, and what makes financial sense.

When to rent a car

  • 1–5 people — Both car classes seat 5 comfortably.
  • Car in the shop — Most affordable way to stay mobile during repairs.
  • Lowest daily rate — Starting at $55/day for local use.
  • Fuel efficiency — Up to 37 MPG on the intermediate car.

Intermediate vs. Full-Size: The intermediate is smaller and more fuel-efficient. The full-size offers more legroom and trunk space. The daily price difference is just $10.

When to rent a minivan (7 passengers)

  • 6–7 people — Keeps everyone in one vehicle without going to a full-size van.
  • Families with kids — Sliding doors, room for car seats, strollers, and luggage.
  • Airport runs — Fits everyone plus luggage for pickups at MSP, La Crosse, or Eau Claire.
  • Insurance replacement for an SUV — Adjusters often authorize a minivan when the damaged vehicle was a larger family car.

When to rent a 15-passenger van

  • 8+ people — One van keeps everyone together.
  • Sports teams — Traveling to tournaments or games with the whole squad.
  • Church groups — Youth retreats, volunteer trips, community outings.
  • Family reunions — Airport pickups or shuttling between event locations.
  • Wedding parties — Moving the bridal party or guests between venues.
  • Tourist groups — ATV trips, State Forest excursions, Driftless Region road trips.

Quick decision guide

  • 1–2 people, short trip → Intermediate Car ($55/day)
  • 3–5 people, road trip or extra comfort → Full-Size Car ($65/day)
  • 6–7 people or family with kids → 7-Passenger Minivan ($78/day)
  • 8+ people, teams, events, reunions → 15-Passenger Van ($135/day)
